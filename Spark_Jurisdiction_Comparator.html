Dubai (UAE) Analysis

License: VARA "Virtual Asset Service Provider"

Why here?

Dubai offers a specific "Rulebook" for tokenization. It is clear, modern, and offers 0% corporate tax in Free Zones (DMCC or DIFC).

The Catch

You must have a physical office and physical employees in Dubai. You cannot be purely virtual. Rent costs ~$20k/year.

Bankability

8/10

Local banks (Emirates NBD) are warming up to VARA licensed firms.

Specific Setup Requirements

  • 1.Register in a Free Zone (e.g., DMCC Crypto Centre).
  • 2.Apply for Initial Approval from VARA (Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority).
  • 3.Hire a local MLRO (Money Laundering Reporting Officer).

Switzerland Analysis

Regulator: FINMA (Financial Market Supervisory Authority)

Why here?

The "Crypto Valley" (Zug) is the most prestigious address in the world. High trust from investors.

The Catch

Extremely Expensive. You need ~100,000 CHF paid-up capital just to incorporate. Legal fees for a "No Action Letter" are massive.

Bankability

9.5/10

Sygnum and SEBA banks are world-class crypto banks.

Singapore Analysis

License: Major Payment Institution (MPI) / CMS

Regulatory Warning

Singapore has recently tightened rules on "Digital Payment Token" (DPT) providers. They actively discourage retail speculation. Unless you are targeting only institutional accredited investors, obtaining a license here is currently considered the hardest path.
